# Calculations

Distance uses Haversine with the IUGG mean Earth radius of 6,371,008.8 metres, only between adjacent points in the same segment. Elapsed duration is the difference between first and last valid source-order timestamps. Moving time is valid, positive-duration track time minus confirmed sustained stops, calculated separately for each segment. A potential stop enters below 1 km/h and is confirmed only after 10 seconds while points remain within 6 metres of its centre. Before confirmation, more than 2 metres of directional drift is treated as continuous slow movement. A confirmed stop exits above 2 km/h or beyond 8 metres; this hysteresis keeps brief slow samples and GPS jitter in moving time. Average speed is total track distance divided by calculated moving time. Maximum speed is the fastest validated centred three-point (normally two-second) estimate: distance from point i - 1 to i + 1 divided by its positive time span. Estimates cannot cross a segment boundary, must not exceed 100 km/h, and speeds above 40 km/h or changing by more than 20 km/h/s need a comparable independent centred estimate to reject isolated spikes. Elevation gain/loss uses a separate accumulated 3-metre threshold in each segment: samples are compared to the last accepted elevation, so gradual climbs are retained while jitter around that anchor is ignored. Both elevation observations are emitted as zero when there are at least two elevation samples in a segment and no accumulated change reaches the threshold; they are omitted when no segment has enough elevation samples. Heart-rate min/max/mean use plausible positive samples.

Geographical bounds include every valid GPX track point. The `schema:GeoShape` `schema:box` is serialized in **south west north east** order: minimum latitude, minimum longitude, maximum latitude, maximum longitude. Start and end locations use the first and last valid points in source traversal order, without sorting or filling missing coordinates.
